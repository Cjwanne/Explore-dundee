About V&A Dundee

V&A Dundee is Scotland's first design museum and the only V&A museum outside London. Designed by internationally acclaimed Japanese architect Kengo Kuma, the building itself is a remarkable feat of design and engineering that appears to hover over the River Tay.

Opening in September 2018, the museum has quickly become an iconic landmark and a symbol of Dundee's cultural renaissance. The building's unique design was inspired by the dramatic cliffs of Scotland's east coast, creating a striking connection between the city and the river.

Exhibitions and Collections

The museum houses Scotland's design heritage, showcasing the country's outstanding achievements in design from the past, present, and future. The museum features:

  • Scottish Design Galleries - Explore 500 years of Scottish design innovation with over 300 objects from the V&A's collections and beyond.
  • Changing Exhibitions - Major international exhibitions that showcase the best of global design.
  • Learning Centre - A dedicated space for design education and community engagement.
  • The Michelin Design Gallery - Showcasing contemporary design talent from Scotland and beyond.

Architecture

The building is an architectural marvel in its own right:

  • Designed by renowned Japanese architect Kengo Kuma
  • Features 2,500 pre-cast concrete panels creating a complex curved façade
  • Extends out over the River Tay, echoing ships docked in the harbor
  • Incorporates sustainable design principles throughout
  • Creates a 'living room for the city' with public spaces accessible to all
