About Dundee Contemporary Arts

Dundee Contemporary Arts (DCA) is an internationally renowned center for contemporary art, cinema, print studio, and creative learning in the heart of Dundee's cultural quarter. Opened in 1999, DCA has become a vital cultural hub for the city and has played a significant role in Dundee's cultural renaissance.

Housed in a converted garage and warehouse, the award-winning building was designed by Richard Murphy Architects and has become one of the city's most distinctive architectural landmarks. The center aims to enrich people's lives through art, culture, and creativity.

What We Offer

Contemporary Art Galleries

DCA presents a diverse program of contemporary art exhibitions featuring world-renowned artists alongside emerging talent. The two gallery spaces host approximately six major exhibitions per year, showcasing everything from painting and sculpture to film, installation, and digital art.

Cinema

Our two-screen cinema shows the best in independent and world cinema, from arthouse films to cult classics and new releases. We also host special screenings, film festivals, and Q&A sessions with filmmakers throughout the year.

Print Studio

One of Scotland's leading print studios, offering facilities for a wide range of printmaking techniques including etching, screenprinting, lithography, and digital printing. The studio welcomes everyone from complete beginners to professional artists.

Create Space

Our dedicated learning space hosts workshops, courses, and activities for all ages. From family drop-in sessions to masterclasses with visiting artists, our Create Space program encourages everyone to get involved with contemporary art.
