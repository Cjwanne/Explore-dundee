About Dundee

Dundee is Scotland's fourth-largest city, located on the north bank of the Firth of Tay. Known as the 'City of Discovery,' Dundee has transformed from an industrial powerhouse to a vibrant cultural hub.

With its stunning waterfront, world-class museums, and friendly locals, Dundee offers visitors an authentic Scottish experience with modern flair.

History of Dundee

Dundee's history spans over 800 years, from its origins as a small settlement to becoming an important port city. The city became known as the center of the "jute, jam and journalism" industries:

  • Jute - Dundee was the world's leading producer of jute products in the 19th century
  • Jam - Famous for Keiller's marmalade, invented in Dundee in the 18th century
  • Journalism - Home to DC Thomson, publisher of iconic comics like The Beano and The Dandy

Plan Your Visit

Dundee is easily accessible by train, bus, and car. The city has a compact center, making it easy to explore on foot.

Best time to visit: May to September offers the best weather with long daylight hours.

Where to stay: From luxury hotels on the waterfront to cozy B&Bs, Dundee offers accommodation for all budgets.

Getting around: Local buses serve all areas of the city, while taxis and ride-sharing services are readily available.
