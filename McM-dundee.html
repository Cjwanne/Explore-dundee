About The McManus

The McManus: Dundee's Art Gallery & Museum is a magnificent Victorian Gothic building that has been at the heart of art and history in the city since 1867. Originally built as a memorial to Prince Albert and known as the Albert Institute, it was renamed in 1984 after its greatest benefactor, Lord Provost Maurice McManus.

Following a major refurbishment completed in 2010, The McManus now offers eight stunning gallery spaces, showcasing Dundee's art, history, and environment through fascinating and diverse exhibitions. The building itself is a remarkable piece of architecture, designed by the renowned Gothic revivalist Sir George Gilbert Scott.

Permanent Collections

The McManus houses an impressive range of permanent collections that tell the story of Dundee and its people from prehistoric times to the present day:

Dundee and the World

Explore Dundee's global connections and the city's role in industry, trade, and exploration. Features artifacts from the city's jute, whaling, and shipbuilding industries.

Victoria Gallery

A stunning display of fine and decorative art from the 19th century to the present day, including works by the Scottish Colourists and the Glasgow Boys.

Landscapes and Lives

Discover the story of life in the Dundee region from prehistoric times to the medieval period, featuring archaeological finds and natural history specimens.

Making of Modern Dundee

Trace the development of Dundee from the 18th century to today, showcasing how the city transformed from a trading port to a center for innovation and culture.

Architectural Highlights

The McManus building is a masterpiece of Gothic Revival architecture, designed by Sir George Gilbert Scott. Completed in 1867, the building features numerous architectural highlights:

Gothic Façade

The striking exterior features pointed arches, ornate tracery, and decorative stonework typical of the Gothic Revival style.

Grand Staircase

The impressive central staircase creates a dramatic entrance to the upper galleries, illuminated by a large stained glass window.

Albert Hall

The magnificent central hall features a hammerbeam roof, beautiful woodwork, and serves as an impressive event space.

Stained Glass

Throughout the building, stunning stained glass windows depict historical scenes and add vibrant color to the interiors.

Gothic Arches

Pointed arches frame doorways and windows throughout the building, creating a distinctive Gothic atmosphere.

Clock Tower

The iconic clock tower rises above the building, serving as a landmark in the city center.
