About RRS Discovery

The Royal Research Ship Discovery is a historic Antarctic exploration vessel and the centerpiece of Dundee's waterfront Discovery Point attraction. Built in Dundee specifically for Antarctic exploration, the RRS Discovery carried Captain Robert Falcon Scott and Ernest Shackleton on their first, groundbreaking journey to Antarctica from 1901-1904, known as the Discovery Expedition.

A Dundee-Built Ship

The RRS Discovery was built in Dundee by the Dundee Shipbuilders Company. Dundee was chosen for the construction due to its expertise in building wooden ships strong enough to navigate the icy waters of the Arctic, gained through the city's long history of whaling.

Key features of the ship include:

  • Specially designed wooden hull with an unconventional rounded bow to ride up over the ice rather than break through it
  • Three masts with both steam and sail capabilities
  • Extra-thick hull reinforced with oak to withstand ice pressure
  • Watertight compartments for safety
  • Custom-built scientific laboratories for the expedition

The Historic Antarctic Expedition

The British National Antarctic Expedition (1901-1904) was the first official British exploration of the Antarctic regions since James Clark Ross's voyage sixty years earlier. The expedition made numerous scientific discoveries and was the first to:

  • Record an Antarctic sledge journey
  • Discover the Antarctic Plateau where the South Pole is located
  • Discover the only snow-free Antarctic valleys
  • Study emperor penguins and their eggs
  • Reach further south than anyone before

Discovery's Journey Through Time

1900-1901

Built in Dundee specifically for Antarctic research

1901-1904

First Antarctic expedition with Captain Scott and Ernest Shackleton

1905-1931

Served as a cargo vessel between London and Hudson Bay

1931-1979

Used as a training ship for Boy Scouts in London

1979-1986

Restoration work undertaken to return the ship to its 1901 condition

1986-Present

Returned to Dundee as a museum ship at Discovery Point
